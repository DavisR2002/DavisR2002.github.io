1.6: Cyber Attacks


Up until now, we have discussed many concepts that surround the idea of digital attacks against computer systems, but have yet to talk about the attacks themselves.

Vulnerable business systems are a prime target for potential cyber-crime. Once an asset has been compromised, a ransom can be demanded of the business in exchange for the asset's safe return. 53% percent of cyber attacks resulted in damages of $500,000 or more.

As the name suggests, a Cyber Attack is an attack carried out across the internet, digitally, by a cyber-criminal. These attacks can vary in their forms and their levels of severity. We'll only cover a few for the purpose of this module.

The top 3 most common cyber attack types are Phishing, Malware and Man in the Middle (MITM) attacks.

Key Points:


1. Cyber attacks are attacks that are carried out over the internet by cyber criminals.

2. Malware describes multiple types of harmful software which can be placed onto a victim's computer systems.

3. Phishing is a technique that involves tricking people into opening harmful files on their devices. These are most commonly seen in the form of convincing emails that look legitimate but actually contain harmful files.

Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) attacks occur when an attacker uses an unsafe digital environment to steal assets during a transaction between two parties. A common environment for this to take place could be a public or otherwise Unsecure WiFi network.
