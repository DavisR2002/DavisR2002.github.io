1.4: Risks


The term Risk in digital security refers to the situation created when the three elements that we have just covered in the last chapter.

A risk comprises of an asset to be negatively affected, an threat actor to carry out this possibility and a vulnerability that can be exploited in order to access the asset in question.

An asset can have vulnerabilities, but no possible threats that could potentially make use of them. Conversely, an asset may have many potential threat actors working against it, with zero vulnerabilities that can be used to gain access. Similarly to the CIA Triad, all three risk conditions must be present in order for a risk to be notable to a system.

In order to determine whether a company's assets are in danger of potential risks, a risk assessment will be carried out. A risk assessment serves to not only evaluate an organization's potential vulnerabilities and threats, but will also include recommended actions that can help to mitigate and reduce the likelihood of risks becoming real problems.

A risk assessment will also state the appropriate Controls (Read on to the next page to learn about Controls) that can be used against any identified risks.

Key Points:


1. Risks are a culmination of a company asset, a threat actor and a present vulnerability. All three are needed to create a significant risk.

2. Risk Assessments are carried out in order to discover potential risks to company assets and nullify them before they can be realized.
