1.3: Assets, Threats and Vulnerabilities


It's very important to understand what a security system should be doing, which we have established in the earlier chapters. It is also just as important, however, to understand what it is you are protecting; your "Assets", what you are trying to avoid; your "Threats", and where the system can be improved to maintain its protective qualities; your "Vulnerabilities".

An asset refers to anything that is of value to a person or organisation. This can be sensitive data, important devices, money and many other forms. An asset container refers to the location where the asset is stored, which is quite an important aspect to consider. Adversely, threats are potential incidents that can cause some kind of negative effect against an asset. Any event that can result in the compromise of an assets's Confidentiality, Integrity or Availabilty can be considered as a threat. Threats can be both deliberate, through criminal means, or accidental, caused by human or technical errors.

Vulnerabilities are flaws in the security system of an organization that can be exploited in order to cause a threat to become realized. Vulnerabilities can be found in software used by an organization that is in need of updating or possesses exploitable bugs in their code. They can also be found in the physical security in place to protect an information asset container. A lack of guarding, cameras or even broken locks or doors can be examples of physical vulnerabilities.

Key Points:


1. Assets are important things, which is why they are the targets of threats. This is made possible through the exploitation of vulnerabilities.

2. A commonly exploited vulnerability is the lack of security in place to guard an asset information container.

3. Using the CIA Triad in order to identify potential threats is a good way to notice unseen vulnerabilities that a security system has.
