1.1: The CIA Triad


The CIA Triad works as a simplistic yet high-level concept reference point, which you can use to evaluate your own security systems.
The CIA Triad has its name due to the three components which make it up: Confidentiality, Integrity and Availabilty. The CIA Triad is not only useful to consider when designing security measures, but is also good to refer to in the aftermath of an unsuccessful defense against a cyber-attack.

For example, following a 'Data Breach' (more on this later) it can be extremely useful to consider whether your current methods, however ineffective at preventing the attack, were able to maintain the confidentiality of important information. This can really help when you have to redesign a security system in these situations, as it can allow you to identify and reuse its current strengths in the new version.
As a rule, any security system in place that does not fufill all three conditions of the Triad are insufficient.
