1.5: Controls


Controls are fundamental components of digital security and risk management. The term describes any policies, techniques, solutions, technologies or actions that can protect an organization's assets.
As we have discussed in the previous section, an aspect of risk assessments are to determine which controls will be most effective at protecting important assets. An important part of this is to analyse how well existing cybersecurity controls have performed.

Controls fall into three catagories: Preventative Controls, which prevent incidents from occuring. Detective Controls, which recognize attacks as they are in progress, then alert security teams. Corrective Controls, which help to minimize the impact from a cyber-attack and return to normality ASAP.

Within those three catagories, there are multiple types of controls: Technical controls, which can include antivirus software, firewalls or software patches. Administrative controls, which can include cybersecurity policies, staff security awareness training, or a clear-desk policy. Physical Controls, which can include locked doors, on-site security teams or biometric scanning.

Key Points:


1. Controls are anything that a company has in place to protect its assets.

2. Controls are mainly organized into three catagories:

  • Preventative Controls

  • Detective Controls

  • Corrective Controls

The catagorization of each control is based on the function that the control is meant to perform.

3. Controls are further organized into three subtypes:

  • Technical

  • Administrative

  • Physical

This is determined by the method that each control uses to protect an organization's assets.
